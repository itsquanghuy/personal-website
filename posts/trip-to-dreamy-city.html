An Unforgettable Trip To A True Dreamy City

Jul. 12, 2023

I wish I stayed here longer.

- I told to myself

Many people find it easy to travel from one city to another city. For me, due to lots of stupid reasons (too cold, too far away, too rainy, etc.), I have had to wait my whole life to finally visit Da Lat city. From the people to the nature, it is difficult to describe how wonderful this city is in only a single word. Here, although I had got sick on the first day because of the temperature difference, I could eventually find the inner peace that I was always looking for for a long time.

How is a small cold city able to retreat a person so well? Let’s explore!

People

Delicate, simple, energetic. I believe that those words express everything about local Da Lat people’s vibes.

The lady in the photo is Mrs. Xuan Nam who opens a small kitchen to serve food cooked by herself. She only takes reservation. Thus, I reserved my spot twice. Why did I do that? Because I always love talking with the local people who know best about their city and the daily life there. What makes her kitchen more special is that her husband, Mr. Duc, plays the piano beautifully. He plays, she cooks. How pleasing it is!

I spent a whole hour only talking with him about music especially the history and the tips and tricks. He had studied engineering but he has a great passion for the piano. The piano helps him balance his life. Moreover, he loves Chopin. I remember seeing his eyes wide open when he talked about the difference between Chopin’s and Beethoven’s music. The conversation ended when his wife called me to have the meal she had prepared for me.

Before showing the meal, I would like to talk about Mrs. Nam’s cooking and serving method. After she confirmed my reservation, she would go to the local market to buy fresh beef, fresh fruits and fresh vegetable. Freshness makes the meal very organic and good for your health especially during a trip, you will eat outside most of the time.

During the cooking time, she would ask me and other people to join the kitchen. Helping her prepare the meal was an unique experience. I believe that she was trying to create a better life for everyone.

When I was having my meal, I had a chance to talk with other people around the table. The person I remember the most was a lady born in rural Hanoi but having been living in Da Lat for more than 10 years. She talked about her career, her life and her mindset. She asked me about my tourism business and recommended me to her friends working in tourism in Da Lat as well, which was very nice of her.

After that, Mrs. Nam, the lady and I exchanged our phone numbers for future meet-up and recommendation. Then, we took some pictures, said goodbye to each other and I continued my journey in Da Lat.

Nature

The nature in Da Lat is quite complex. It could be peaceful, calm, or aggressive at the same time.

This is Tuyen Lam Lake. It was serene on a rainy day. I parked my scooter near the lake. I walked around and used any protection I had to keep my camera dry and I started shooting. Because of the breathtaking sightseeing, I only wished that the film price had been low so that I could buy more film rolls to shoot more.

Turn around and drive forward, I visited Datanla Waterfall. The waterfall was so aggressive that the droplets could have damaged my camera any time if I stayed close to it. Thus, I walked back a bit far so there were no droplets being able to touch my camera. Then, I shot and the photo came into my life.

Cafés

I visited 3 famous vintage cafés in Da Lat. I only took photos at 2 of them because I had to save my film for later.

Duong

My first impression when arriving at this café was that I felt warm. It is a cozy café with a big bookshelf on the garret.

I ordered one season-based ice cream which was made depending on the current season. It was the season of plums so that they made me a plum ice cream. Sitting on the garret, enjoying my ice cream and did some research on how to extend my photography Instagram to more deep insightful content. It was this very café that made me decide to write blogs about photography and life on Medium.

Tung

I know this vintage café from the movie “Em va Trinh”. It is about the life story of a musician called “Vietnamese Bob Dylan” Trinh Cong Son. We, Vietnamese people, love his songs. Romantic, deep and sometimes sad.

After watching the movie, I read more about the café’s history. I was fascinated that it was founded in around 1940. It has been the place for artists to gather there and let the atmosphere bring them to their highest creative state.

Entering the café and the atmosphere was probably suitable for the artist. I ordered one original yogurt. It is extremely difficult to find a handmade original yogurt. People in Da Lat call it “Yao gua”. I have no idea why they call it because in Hanoi, we call it a completely different name in Vietnamese.

While I was tasting my “Yao gua”, they played some Andy Williams’ songs. That good old songs – “Love story”, “Speak softly love”, etc.. It was simple yet elegant.

Restaurants

Beside Mrs. Nam’s kitchen, I ate at many other restaurants but I only took photos from one because of the same reason, film shortage!

I tried Banh Canh which was noodles with fish sausage, fried onion and some fresh vegetable. It was not tasty and too spicy for me.

For the others, some give me too much protein (a lof of meat), some give me too much fat. Hence, no restaurants other than Mrs. Nam’s kitchen stay in my head.

Local Market

An interesting market. It is always crowded especially at night, which is the main reason of the traffic jam near the market. I buy nothing. I only walked around to see the market from my point of view.

There is a famous spot called “Hong Kong spot” where the teenagers and the youth love selfies because the background looks like Hong Kong in the 1990s. I found a hard time to find a way to the spot. I had to try so many stairs, sometimes got stuck and had to go back. Finally, I made it and it was worth the try. I did not have any models so instead of taking photos straight ahead, I moved my camera down and captured the lively street below. It was so bustle.

Then, I “shot” the people around me using Joel Meyerowitz style. Some people only passed by, some people stopped and took a photo, some people stopped as well but talked to each other. What a mixture of people!

Wrap-up

In conclusion, I had a big crush on Da Lat. Cold yet comfortable weather. Lovely people. Beautiful scenary. I assure that I will come back there one day. There are so many beautiful memories which is helpful for my well-being. See you again, Da Lat!
