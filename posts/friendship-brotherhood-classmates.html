Friendship, Brotherhood, Classmates

Aug. 7, 2023

Throughout a human life, 12 years is a relatively big number for not only a friendship but also brotherhood. These two classmates’ relationship duration is not an exception.

Introduction to the brotherhood

Although I have already known that guy since primary school, it was not until grade 7 that we finally met at an English extra course. During the course, it was humorous that our teacher always mispronounced his name. Meanwhile, my classmates called me “grade 6’s gangster” because I attended the wrong class. Since then, I have been calling him by that name every time I want to tease him.

After the English course, we had not met each other for 3 years. At the beginning of grade 10, we coincidently attended the same high school and the same class. I then walked toward him and asked whether his mispronounced name was correct or not. He replied: “You are the grade 6’s gangster”. It was the beginning of a beautiful friendship.

I could not emphasize enough that high school was the most wonderful time in my life. That guy played an important part as well. In grade 10, he showcased his guitar skills to the class. He told me that he had been studying for 3 years at that time. I loved the song “Forever” by Stratovarious. I asked him to play but he never played it in spite of knowing how to play that song. Therefore, in grade 11, I decided to learn how to play the guitar. He recommended me to his guitar teacher. At that point, he thought I only lasted a few months but it turned out that I had studied extremely hard to be able to play a complete song after a year. Without guitar, I believe my life would be different. It calms me down and brings the romanticism in me out to the world. I am thankful to him for introducing me to such a great teacher with whom I had studied for another 5 years.

Moreover, that guy loves poetry and literature but he studied in a scientific-based class. Therefore, many weird funny things happened. Because of many male students in our scientific class, he would sometimes try to intimidate some of them especially me by holding our hand and reading the “I love you” poem by Alexander Pushkin to us. At first, we were shocked. We tried to push his hand away from us. However, we later understood that it was his joke so we also learned the poem by heart. Then, every time he held our hand, we would read it with him. What a parody we have got then! It is the only poem I remember until this day.

When we had to study hard for high school graduation and university entrance in grade 12, we were one of the few students doing homework handed by our school teacher. We still do not understand why we did that while other students did homework by their extra course teacher, not our school teacher. We laughed about it every time we talked about it.

Darkest time

We, of course, had conflicts. It happened mostly during university. We did not attend the same class with the same people week by week like in high school because we studied different majors at different universities. Different majors and universities come with different perspectives. The distance cracks the relationship as well. I believe when there are not many conversations, it is hard to get close to the people you know. However, one thing I learned from that time is that when people have built a pure friendship, they will find a way to resolve any conflicts and distances. So did we.

My bro’s marriage

That guy told me that he had a girlfriend a year ago. They had been friends for a year and a half before. He and I scheduled many times so that he could introduce his girlfriend to me in person but we failed due to many reasons (rain, sickness, etc.). Fast forward to this Lunar New Year, he surprised me that he would marry his girlfriend, which caused me a night without good sleep. He would be her husband. Finally, I had a chance to meet his fiancée after Lunar New Year’s Eve at his house. After a few talks did I realize that she was the woman to whom he had to get married.

Before the wedding photobook shooting session, I asked them to become their deputy film photographer. They accepted. However, life cannot be more cruel to me. I broke my camera and got COVID-19 one week before the shooting day. Fortunately, she lent me her film camera but hers was a viewfinder while mine was a rangefinder. I recovered from COVID-19 just two days before as well.

During the session, I was extremely nervous because I did not get used to her camera. Nevertheless, somehow they trusted me with all their guts. I managed to shoot with all the knowledge I had of the viewfinder. In the end, their trust paid off. Although half of the roll was a waste, the others were a masterpiece. At first, I set the shutter speed too low causing the photos blurry and overexposed. Later, I doubled the shutter speed using my gut feeling and got the perfect results.

On their wedding day, this time I bought a new camera with an automatic light meter. The light meter does save me a lot of money on film. It frees me from wasting time light-metering the environment to focus entirely on the upcoming moment. Thus, their film wedding photos were exceptional. 36 exposures results in 36 beautiful lively pictures. I have saved their memory. It was pure, lovely, and imperfect. They hired professional photography videography staff to capture and record their perfect moments with a telelens and a gimbal. I myself embraced the imperfection for them with handheld recording and some shaky film photos.

I hope that I help them fulfill their love desire. After all, we are close friends. We do not seek perfection. We seek purity, love, and humanity in our brotherhood. I believe that it matters the most.

The Future

I look forward to the future they build together. I know that they always have my back in case they need my help.

From the bottom of my heart, I love you guys purely.

- Huy
