Finding Peace

Jul. 14, 2023

Working comes with resting. I believe the equation always needs balancing. Choosing to visit Vung Tau City was a wise choice giving me the necessary break from work. Vung Tau is not a fancy city with not many tall buildings and not many famous traditional foods. It is best known for the slow calm atmosphere to which brings people coming. The sea, the mountain, the people. I learned how to feel every single moment when living in this city.

I did not take a food photo. This city is not famous for the food as described. What I had taken were the people and the sightseeing.

People

I stayed at my aunt’s house. When I arrived, my nephew ran towards me and hugged me tight. Later, we found out that she mismatched me with my aunt’s son who looks mostly like me. That is why she hugged me so tight. We laughed a lot about that. I still love her as well.

Beside the mismatch, my nephew and I were close. She loved my analog camera, Canon Canonet G-III QL17. She was curious about why she could not see the photos after taken. I explained to her why the film camera was different but she hardly understood it. Generation gap came into real life clearly at that point. I took some photos of her using Fuji C200 roll to produce beautiful color in those. The film roll did not disappoint me at all. My nephew looked beautiful, playful and lovely in the frames.

When I went outside, I ran into a park in which people workouted or played Chinese chess. I stopped, grabbed my camera and started shooting. It was the first time I used my new camera so that there was only one good photo from those shots. In this one, two men were having a tense match. One man saw me and waved his hand to fuck me off but I captured the right moment so that no angry gesture had shown in the photo.

Sightseeing

My aunt introduced me a local young man to be my “tour guide” on my first day in Vung Tau as well. He is 2 years younger than me. He is a good humble young man. After taking photos in the park, I went home and waited for the local man.

When he arrived at my aunt’s house, we introduced ourselves. It was late afternoon so that he drove me to a coast where I could take some sunset photos. Unfortunately, there was no sunset at all. However, the scenary was so breathtaking that I could still catch some beautiful moments.

This is an example from those beautiful shots. When I moved my camera down, I saw a lovely couple sitting near the wave-cut cliff and talking to each other. I “shot”, of course. What a combination of privacy and immensity!

We went down to the berm. Walking along, there was a scooter that someone had parked it and gone fishing. It looked lonely. Together with the cloud behind and the Fuji C200 film roll, the photo truly expresses its loneliness.

In the next morning, I rode my aunt’s motorcycle back to the coast which my “tour guide” and I had visited to catch the sunrise. I was lucky this time. It was not cloudy. The sun rose higher and higher. The hard light could not stop me from shooting continuously.

Street photography is great for that you do not need a model to have a good photo. You look around. If any person is perfectly in your frame, you click. I believe I learn that lesson by my heart.

Final words

I could not say when I am going to visit Vung Tau once again. Nevertheless, I do know that it has a special place in my memory. I retreated myself well when staying in this city thanks to its atmosphere. Hence, One day, Vung Tau!
