The Excitement Of Getting The First Film Scan

Jul. 19, 2023

I love film photography. The grain and the film color make me goosebumps. I have been taking many film photos since 2021 after my colleague introduced me to his camera. Every time I bring my roll to the lab, go home, and wait for the result, the same feeling grows in my body as the day to receive the scan is close. I am always nervous yet excited to see my accomplishment. However, the nervousness and the excitement were even bigger the first time I looked forward to getting my first one. The reason for an explanation is simple. It was a mess.

At first, my colleague taught me some fundamental knowledge about photography such as ISO, shutter speed, and aperture. I must say I did not understand anything. Hence, he suggested me to download a light meter app on my phone. Life was much easier. Then, I brought his camera to the resting room and set the camera up with the settings the app metered for me. I started shooting. The photos were great at this point. I was happy.

My happiness stayed not for too long, I kicked off messing up. Life was not easy at all, especially for an adventurous person like me. After taking quality photos using the meter app, I wanted to be more professional. I dropped off my phone. I let my instinct tell me what settings need changing. However, my instinct was terrible. Even nowadays, my instinct is quite bad as well. When sending an email with an attached scan, the lab told me that some were too overexposed or underexposed that they cannot see the result. The photo above was the least underexposed that they could return to me.

Another mistake I made was to open the film camera’s back. I opened it many times until another colleague warned me not to do it because it would expose the film to the light and ruin everything. Fortunately, only a few photos were “too overexposed” and I could still see the others with special exposing effects.

In general, it was not a big failure. I still had some great photos to post on my Instagram. I had a chance to fall in love with this amazing kind of photography because I am an indie man and I love vintage stuff.
