100% Focusing On Film Photography

Jul. 18, 2023

I love capturing moments. I believe that the more years to come, the more valuable a photo will be. However, when I look back my previous photos from 2017, most of them taken by my phone do not contain any value at all. There is no riddle needing an answer. Meanwhile, I have started taking film photos since 2021. Excluding some failed shots, most of my successful film photos stay and give the readers some meanings from them. Those clues make make me think about why I captured many bad invaluable photos back then and how I could get the most valuable stories out of my photos.

Suddenly, I realize that I take photos too fast using my phone or my digital camera. I do not let my eye see, my mind think and my heart beat before clicking. When my heart does not beat, how do I expect my readers to feel what my heart wants to tell them if it does not say anything at all? Therefore, I decided to put my Moment phone lens in my closet, return my digital camera back to my father and buy a new film camera with an automatic light meter. I archived all my photos on Instagram which are not film as well. I force myself to really see and feel the surrounding before I put my camera on, adjust the focus and click. It actually worked. Together with the vintage film color, my recent photos look deeper and more meaningful, which gives the readers a riddle needing an answer. Above all, I do slow myself down and have an opportunity to think of what is worth being in my photos.
