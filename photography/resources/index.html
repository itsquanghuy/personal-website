My Resources

I've experimented with various photography tools, initially using smartphone lenses and ultimately embracing a film camera. I've discovered a sense of contentment with the film camera, despite the inherent graininess and halation it brings to my photos.

Canonet GIII QL17

This camera is like a budget-friendly Leica alternative. It takes sharp photos, even in low light with its f/1.7 aperture, and the automatic light meter helps me capture fast-paced street scenes effectively.

Kodak Colorplus

The film produces a striking contrast, often accentuated by a rich gold color tone. I adore how this high contrast effect sharpens the focus on my intended subject, making it stand out prominently in the photo.

Kodak Ultramax

This camera's high ISO and bold contrast, along with its unique pink tone, prove invaluable for shooting in tough conditions like fog and low light. It ensures clear and captivating shots in limited visibility.

Kodak Vision 250D

Despite being budget-friendly, this film roll produces exceptional photos with a captivating green tone that adds a cinematic touch. Its ISO range from 250 to 400 enables versatile shooting in different lighting conditions.
